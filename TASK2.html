The Journey of Coffee

Coffee's origins trace back to 9th-century Ethiopia, where a goatherd named Kaldi noticed his goats becoming energized after eating red berries from a specific plant. These berries made their way to Yemen, where Sufi monks brewed them into a drink that fueled their prayers. By the 15th century, coffee spread across the Arabian Peninsula, later captivating Europe in the 17th century. Its unique aroma and stimulating effects soon revolutionized trade and culture, leading to coffeehouses becoming centers of social and intellectual exchange. Today, coffee remains a beloved global tradition, symbolizing connection and comfort.

The Story of Starbucks

Starbucks was founded in 1971 in Seattle's Pike Place Market by three partners: Jerry Baldwin, Zev Siegl, and Gordon Bowker. Initially, it focused on selling high-quality coffee beans and equipment rather than brewed coffee. Inspired by the maritime culture of Seattle, the founders chose the name "Starbucks" from Moby Dick and adopted a mermaid logo symbolizing the allure of the sea. Over time, under Howard Schultz's leadership, Starbucks evolved into a global coffeehouse phenomenon, redefining the coffee experience and becoming a cultural icon.
